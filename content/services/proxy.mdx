## Legal Framework

### Power of Attorney

For some services, we use limited power of attorney:

- **Scope**: Specific to the service (e.g., "datacenter operations")
- **Duration**: Typically 12-month renewable
- **Limitations**: Explicit bounds on authority
- **Revocable**: You can revoke at any time

### Agency Agreement

Our standard agreement includes:

- **Fiduciary duty**: We act in your best interest
- **Confidentiality**: All your information is protected
- **Liability**: Clear allocation of responsibilities
- **Dispute resolution**: Mediation and arbitration clauses

### Ownership Structure

**Option 1: Direct Agency**
- We own assets legally
- You have contractual rights to use and control
- Simplest structure, fastest setup

**Option 2: LLC Proxy**
- We form an LLC
- You're the beneficial owner
- We're the registered agent/manager
- More protection, more complexity

**Option 3: Trust Structure**
- Assets held in trust
- You're the beneficiary
- We're the trustee
- Maximum protection, highest cost

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is this legal?**
A: Yes! Agency relationships and proxy arrangements are well-established in law. We structure everything properly with appropriate documentation.

**Q: What happens if you disappear?**
A: Our agreements include succession plans and your rights are documented. You can access everything independently if needed.

**Q: Can I switch proxy providers later?**
A: Yes, though there may be complexity in transferring some assets. We help facilitate transitions.

**Q: How do taxes work?**
A: Depends on structure. We file required returns for our entities. You may have tax obligations depending on beneficial ownership structure. Consult a tax advisor.

**Q: What if there's a dispute?**
A: Our agreements include mediation and arbitration clauses. We also carry E&O insurance for protection.

**Q: Can you act as proxy for regulated industries?**
A: Some industries (finance, healthcare, etc.) have special requirements. We evaluate case-by-case.

**Q: How do I know you won't abuse this?**
A: Limited authority scope, audit trails, insurance, reputation, and legal recourse. Start small, build trust, scale up.
